# ROCKY SAVES THE UNIVERSE — the bible

## The pitch

*Project Hail Mary* from the other side. You are Rocky: an Eridian engineer, blind,
five-legged, from a world of hot ammonia at twenty-nine atmospheres where nobody has
ever seen anything. Your star is going out. Nobody knows why. You will end up
forty-two light years from home, alone, in a ship full of dead friends, trying to
talk to something with a face.

## The one mechanic everything is built on

**Sound is the world, and sound is the puzzle.**

The engine floods the voxel grid with a wavefront every time anything makes a noise.
Air is cheap to cross. Rock is dear. **Grit is nearly soundproof. Xenonite is nearly
free.** That single table — cost per material — turns the sonar into a puzzle
substrate:

* A **resonator** is a listener. It opens a door when enough sound *reaches* it.
* So a locked door is not a key hunt. It is a routing problem: **get a noise to that
  listener.** Walk closer and shout. Cut a channel. Bridge the gap with xenonite,
  which conducts. Pack the wrong path with grit, which does not. Drop a heavy block
  to make the noise for you. Open a vent and let the machine do it.
* Every one of those is legal. That is the Portal promise: the room states a fact
  about physics, and you are trusted to abuse it.

Nothing here is a special case in code. The resonator asks the same field the screen
draws. **The rule and the picture are the same object.**

## The six acts

Each act is ~6–8 levels. A level teaches ONE idea, then the next one combines it with
everything before. No level ever requires a verb it has not taught.

### ACT I — THE COLD *(shipped)*
Erid. The warren. Rocky's own workshop.
1. **The Cold** — the gauges have all fallen. It is not a broken vent. It is the sky. ✅
2. The Deep Hall — climb; the warren is bigger than you thought.
3. Consensus — Eridians have no government and no war. To act, forty-one engineers
   must *agree*. You go and convince them, one at a time, in sound.
4. The Astronomers — a species that has never seen its star measures it by warmth.
   You build the instrument that proves the dimming is real.
5. The Petrova Line — something is EATING the light.

### ACT II — THE SHIP
A species with no metallurgy problem, no concept of vacuum, and no idea what a star
looks like, building a starship anyway.
1. **Xenonite** — the material that made you possible. Carry it. Place it. It sings.
2. The Hull — pressure. Erid is 29 atm; space is zero. Your ship must hold *in*, not
   keep out.
3. The Drive — astrophage. It is killing your star, so you will ride it.
4. The Volunteers — you are chosen. So are twenty-two others. They will all die.
5. Launch — you cannot see the sky you are leaving.

### ACT III — THE LONG DARK
Forty-two light years, and no windows, because windows are for eyes.
1. Sleep — the crew rotation. Time dilation, honestly modelled.
2. The Failure — the ship is dying and so is everyone on it.
3. **Alone** — Rocky is the last one. This is the quiet middle of the game and it
   should hurt.
4. Tau Ceti — arrival. Something else is here.

### ACT IV — THE OTHER
Something has come to the same dying star, and it is doing something inexplicable.
1. The Blip — a contact that does not answer.
2. Approach — it moves like nothing you have ever built.
3. **The Airlock** — the first meeting. It is a shape, and it does something with a
   flat panel of its head that you cannot hear at all.
4. Twenty-nine atmospheres — the thing will die in your ship. You will die in its.
   Neither of you can breathe the other's air, and you both know it inside a minute.

### ACT V — THE WORD *(the heart of the whole game)*
Two creatures with nothing in common but arithmetic. The genuinely great chapter, and
the reason to make this game at all.
1. **Numbers** — you both count. It counts in ten, which is insane. You count in six.
2. Names — pointing at things until a word sticks.
3. The Wall — build the xenonite tunnel between the ships, so you can be in the same
   room without dying.
4. **Question** — the first time either of you asks something rather than states it.
5. Grief — you tell it about your dead. It tells you about its.

### ACT VI — THE FIX, AND THE WAY HOME
1. Taumoeba — the answer was alive all along.
2. Breeding — it must eat astrophage and survive your air AND its air. Two worlds,
   one bug.
3. The Betrayal of Physics — the bug leaks through xenonite. Everything you built.
4. **The Turn** — your friend is dying and is going the wrong way. You have fuel. You
   have a choice, and the game does not make it for you.
5. Home — Erid, warm again. Adrian, warm again. You never see either of them.

## Rules of the writing

* **Rocky speaks in chords.** You always see the chord and the translation. He is
  funny, blunt, and enormously kind. He says "question" as a whole word.
* **The science is real.** Astrophage, the Petrova line, relativity, xenonite,
  taumoeba, base six, 29 atmospheres, 210 °C, no eyes. Where the book is right, be
  right.
* **Never narrate what the player can hear.** If it matters, put it in the world and
  let them pulse at it.
* **No level is a corridor with a key in it.** Every locked thing is a routing problem
  with more than one answer.
